# Dossier Language Style Guide

This guide captures the best language patterns from the stronger Arsenal, Liverpool, Manchester United, Barcelona, and Real Madrid dossier exports, and turns them into rules for the human-facing dossier layer.

The goal is simple:
- keep the confidence and cadence of the best older dossiers
- remove filler, hallucinated specifics, and corporate fluff
- make the output readable to an operator without flattening the strategic tone

## What Worked

The best sections used a repeating structure:
- `Overall Assessment`
- `Yellow Panther Opportunity`
- `Recommended Approach`
- `Introduction Paths`

That structure works because it answers four questions in order:
- what is this entity now
- where is the opportunity
- how should Yellow Panther approach it
- how do we get in

The strongest writing also did three things well:
- it named a real operating reality, such as `Arteta`, `PSR`, `Hale End`, `Adidas`, or `Commercial / Innovation`
- it converted that reality into an implication
- it ended with a practical action

Example of the good shape:
- Arsenal is in a transition from prudence to investment.
- That makes performance-linked, revenue-adjacent pilots the cleanest entry point.
- Start with a proof of value in performance analysis, then expand into commercial activation.

## Approved Patterns

Use these sentence patterns.

### 1. Thesis First
Start with one clear claim about the entity.

Good:
- `Arsenal is moving from financial prudence to strategic investment.`
- `Liverpool is struggling with a fragmented market narrative.`
- `Real Madrid operates in a high-value niche where exclusivity matters.`

### 2. Evidence to Implication
Use a specific fact, then explain what it means.

Good:
- `Because the club is data-driven, the cleanest entry point is performance tooling rather than broad brand messaging.`
- `Because decision-making is centralized, cold outreach will underperform compared with a warm channel.`

### 3. Actionable Ending
End each paragraph with a concrete next step.

Good:
- `Lead with a proof of value in load management.`
- `Approach via the commercial or innovation team.`
- `Use the sponsor pathway before the direct club route.`

### 4. Controlled Modality
Use `likely`, `appears`, `suggests`, and `points to` when the evidence is incomplete.

Good:
- `This likely points to a pilot-first motion.`
- `The structure suggests a commercial gatekeeper model.`

## Banned Patterns

Avoid these patterns.

### 1. Generic Business Slop
Do not use phrases like:
- `strategic accelerator`
- `future-ready`
- `high-velocity`
- `synergy`
- `world-class`
- `unlocking value`
- `innovation-first`

These words are only allowed if they are tied to a real observed fact.

### 2. Unsupported Specifics
Do not invent:
- revenue numbers
- named executives
- partnerships
- product launches
- internal programs

If a fact is not verified, keep it abstract or mark it as likely.

### 3. Overwritten Metaphor
The `Yellow Panther` framing should support the sentence, not dominate it.

Bad:
- `Yellow Panther should become a beacon of predatory agility and invisible security.`

Better:
- `Yellow Panther should lead with operational agility and a narrow, testable pilot.`

### 4. Long Multi-claim Paragraphs
Do not stack five claims in one paragraph.

Prefer:
- one thesis
- one supporting reason
- one action

## Preferred Section Templates

### Overall Assessment
Use 2 to 4 sentences.

Template:
- what the entity is doing now
- what pressure it is under
- why that matters for Yellow Panther

### Yellow Panther Opportunity
Use 2 to 4 sentences.

Template:
- where Yellow Panther fits
- what problem it should attack first
- why that problem is commercially useful

### Recommended Approach
Use 2 to 4 sentences.

Template:
- pilot-first or partner-first motion
- who to approach
- what the first proof should demonstrate

### Introduction Paths
Use 1 to 3 sentences.

Template:
- the best route in
- the gatekeeper or intermediary
- why direct outreach is or is not optimal

## Arsenal Example

### Strong version
`Arsenal is moving from financial prudence to strategic investment. The cleanest Yellow Panther entry point is a performance-linked pilot that proves value before asking for broader adoption. Approach through the commercial or innovation path, then expand once the pilot has evidence.`

### Weak version
`Arsenal is a high-velocity, strategic accelerator opportunity with huge upside. Yellow Panther should leverage its unique value proposition to unlock next-gen innovation and drive transformation.`

### Why the strong version works
- it names a real operating condition
- it ties the condition to a route in
- it gives a sequencing rule
- it avoids inflated language

## How To Apply This In The System

### Human-facing dossier layer
Use the style guide here.
- concise
- decisive
- evidence-aware
- readable by an operator

### Ralph/system layer
Keep the system layer tighter and more structured.
- section state
- confidence
- freshness
- evidence refs
- next action

### Graph and freshness layer
Keep graph-backed language specific and short.
- what changed
- when it changed
- what relationships matter now

## Editing Rule

Before adding any new dossier prose, ask:
1. Is this fact verified?
2. Does this sentence change the operator’s next decision?
3. Can it be shorter without losing meaning?

If the answer to all three is not yes, cut it.
